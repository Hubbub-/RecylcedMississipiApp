ABOUT

PROJECT

We are on a quest to travel the length of the second longest river in the USA on a boat made from recycled and reclaimed materials. Our goal is to produce a documentary. Its purpose is to follow the challenges of a journey on trash, to unearth the reason behind why the Mississippi River is one of the most polluted in the world, and to tell the story of how local communities along the river have been affected by its pollution and their efforts to clean and restore it. The film will be submitted to film festivals and be made freely available to teachers and students around the world to help educate about giving waste a second life and protecting our waterways.

MEET THE TEAM

EXPEDITION LEADER

Dan has been leading 'recycled expeditions' on kayaks made from plastic bottles in his spare time for the past three years. GARY BENCHEGHIB

FILMMAKER / PRODUCER

Gary is a filmmaker and environmental activist. Witnessing his home island of Bali change from a tropical paradise to a congested playground for the world's economic elite, with trash overflowing it's once picturesque beaches, he founded Make A Change Bali at 14-years old: a non-profit organization aimed at educating the local community about the effects of plastic waste and the importance of sustainable waste management. His latest project The Reclamation is a documentary exploring Tolak Reklamasi, one of the biggest environmental movements in the history of Indonesia, reach over 3 million people and obtaining write ups including Rolling Stone Magazine, CNN, and ABC Radio Australia.

PAST EXPEDITIONS

ABEL TASMAN

NEW ZEALAND

Our most recently expedition saw 25 youth travel along the coastline of one of New Zealand’s most treasured National Parks, the Abel Tasman.

  Participants learnt about the Department of Conservation’s work to rejuvenate native bird populations and reduce the prevalence of pests. Local guides shared indigenous perspectives of environmental protection through history and cultural lessons highlighting the flora and fauna that are at most risk to the adverse effects of climate change.

Our pilot science and sustainability curriculum delivered online and through our schools tour were used by over 2,000 students around the world. Participants of the expedition were trained and equipped to deliver presentations in their local communities. Videos and blogs of the expedition were reported on by every national media outlet and channel in New Zealand as a leading example of youth innovation and creativity to inspire and educate the next generation on environmental issues; reaching and engaging over 2 million people.

WHANGANUI RIVER

NEW ZEALAND

Our first expedition in 2013 saw 25 youth travel 100kms down the Whanganui, New Zealand’s longest navigable river. Participants learnt about the disastrous effects of farming and pesticides in contaminating the river and the flow-on effects further downstream. Fascinating parts of New Zealand’s culture and history were explored through experiencing man-made phenomenons such as the famous Bridge-to-Nowhere designed for trade but eventually abandoned in the middle of the New Zealand wilderness. 

Many of the participants of the Whanganui expedition were involved in the design and construction of the plastic bottle kayaks, with members going on to organise and lead the expedition to the Abel Tasman in 2015. 

The expedition garnered significant interest from national media outlets with over 1.5 million people reached. Requests for design blueprints of our kayaks were received from countries around the world.
